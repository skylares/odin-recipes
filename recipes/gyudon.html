Gyudon 牛丼

Description

No dish seems as ubiquitous throughout Japanese food culture as Gyudon. Gyudon is simple, like much of Japan's culinary mainstays, yet packs impressive flavour. In simple terms, gyudon is a beef bowl; thinly sliced beef cooked in a delicious sauce sitting on top of a steaming bowl of rice, topped with perfectly cooked egg, green onions, and ginger.

Ingredients

Serves 2

  • 1 white onion
  • 2 green onions
  • 3/4 lb thinly sliced beef
  • 1 Tbsp canola oil
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 2 Tbsp sake
  • 2 Tbsp mirin
  • 3 Tbsp soy sauce

Steps

  1. Thinly slice the white onion and green onions, keep separate.
  2. Add the oil to a pan over medium-high heat. Once shimmering, add the white onion.
  3. Cook, stiring frequently enough to prevent burns until the onions are lightly browned.
  4. Add the sugar, sake, mirin, and soy sauce to the pan. Stir and allow to cook for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Add the beef, stir to evenly distribute throughout the sauce.
  6. While the beef cooks, crack the eggs into a bowl and lightly whisk them. You do not want a perfectly homogenenous mixture.
  7. Once the beef only has a little red left showing, pour the egg mixture on top and cover the pan. Cook for about 5 minutes, or until the egg is set enough for your liking. If you trust your eggs, a bit runny is okay!
  8. Put the beef mixture on freshly cooked white rice, and top with your green onions.
  9. Enjoy!
